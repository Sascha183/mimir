Computers feel mysterious. They play games, recognize faces, hold conversations. It's natural to assume something complicated must be going on inside.

The truth is the opposite. A computer can do only a handful of very simple things — and it can do only one of them at a time. What makes computers feel powerful is that they do these tiny things hundreds of millions of times per second, one after another.

Everything you'll learn in this module is about those small operations: what they are, how they're built, and how stacking them up produces a working machine. You'll start with a single electrical switch and work your way up to a CPU that can run a program.

## What this isn't

This is not a course on programming, on using a particular operating system, or on the history of computing. It's about the physical machine: what's inside the box and how it actually works.
